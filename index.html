About

Hunter Value Capital manages a single investment strategy.

We perform fundamental research on publicly traded companies and use this research to build portfolios that are concentrated in our best ideas. Our primary focus is the long-term preservation of capital, while seeking to compound capital at above-average net returns over a full business cycle.

Philosophy

As long-term investors, we begin with the premise a stock is a piece of a business--and the underlying value per share of that business will eventually be reflected in the stock price. We seek businesses that have three primary characteristics:

Moat

Returns on capital for companies with sustainable competitive advantages -or "economic moats”-take a long time to slowly decline due to competitive forces, because such firms are able to keep competitors at bay for a longer time. Companies with moats are more likely to increase their intrinsic value over time, and that growth in intrinsic value protects the value of the shares. Companies with structural advantages are also more resilient when faced with economic slowdowns or temporary market trouble. We ask ourselves “What prevents a smart, well-financed competitor from taking market share from this company?” To answer this, we examine the sources of a company's moat, including cost and size advantages, intangible assets, network effects and switching costs - and the likelihood that they will persist over the long term.

Owner-managers

An owner-manager has a huge economic incentive to grow the business over the next decade due to their large ownership share. The best way a CEO can be truly aligned with minority shareholders is by owning a share of the stock that is significant relative to the CEO’s current income and to his/her estimated net worth. We also look for integrity, capital allocation acumen, competitive drive and operational excellence.

Growth

We prefer to invest in businesses which can reinvest their profits into the business at high incremental rates of return. A company’s ability to compound its value per share over long periods is often overlooked by market participants with a short-term focus. Our long-term focus enables us to harness underappreciated sources of growth within a business, including gaining market share in fragmented industries, expanding into newly formed or adjacent markets, improving the business in ways that increase pricing power. Therefore, we seek to identify the intrinsic value growth potential of a business based on a thorough assessment of its qualitative attributes.
